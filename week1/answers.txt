Exercise 1.1: Safety vs. Security:

Safety means preventing the model from risks that caused by unintentional failures. 
Example: If the model fails to detect the traffic signal as Green due the foggy weather, it will lead to collision with the other vehicles or the Pedestrians.

Security means pretending the model from risks that caused by any intentional attacks. 
Example:  an attacker could manipulate input images so that the model misclassifies a traffic signal or pedestrian, leading to unsafe decisions.

Exercise 1.2:

1) There are mainly four types of risk categories in the EU AI act

a)Unacceptable risks: The risk which are non negotiable, for example collecting the biometric in public, collecting the images of the people in public places.

b) High risk: The models which are requires more attention in training, testing and monitoring. For eg, autonomous vehicle where the model failure leads to severe damage.

c) Limited Risk: The model which requires transparency, for example suggestions of ads through the search history.

d) Minimal risk: The model does not require any safety/security measures. For example, developing games with AI bots.

2) a) An autonomous vehicle will be categorized under the High risk, as we need more attention in model training, testing and monitoring to reduce the high life risks. 
   b) Chat bot comes under limited risk category, though it is less in risk, It requires transparency  so that people can understand that they are interacting with the AI systems.

Exercise 1.5:

i)Testing helps evaluate the model’s performance and ensures that it makes correct predictions under normal conditions.

ii) Explain ability helps understand why the model makes certain predictions and ensures that it relies on meaningful features.

iii) Uncertainty estimation helps determine how confident the model is in its predictions, allowing safer decisions when confidence is low.

iv) Anomaly detection helps identify unusual or unseen inputs that may lead to unreliable predictions.

v) Adversarial machine learning helps identify and defend against malicious attacks that can manipulate model predictions.

vi) Alignment ensures that the model’s behavior matches the intended goals and avoids harmful or unintended actions.
